# Platform Capabilities, Limitations & Analytical Boundaries

This document details the functional capabilities and boundaries of **Cgl Regulation Explorer (v0.2.0)**. Users should review these guidelines before interpreting analysis outputs or drawing biological conclusions.

---

## 1. Active Simulation Capabilities (FBA / FVA / ecFBA / rFBA)

When the local FastAPI backend is running (default port 8000), the platform supports active, numerical metabolic simulations:
* **Flux Balance Analysis (FBA)**: Computes steady-state metabolic flux distributions and growth rates using the core *iCW773* model.
* **Flux Variability Analysis (FVA)**: Computes the minimum and maximum feasible flux ranges under baseline and mutant states.
* **Dynamic regulatory FBA (rFBA)**: Simulates substrate consumption (glucose), biomass growth, and product secretion (glutamate) over time-courses, integrating kinetic ordinary differential equations with regulatory Boolean states.
* **Enzyme-Constrained FBA (ecFBA)**: Solves optimization problems incorporating total macromolecular protein pool limits ($P_{\mathrm{pool}}$) and Arrhenius-based temperature scaling of enzyme turnover numbers ($k_{\mathrm{cat}}$), predicting exact metabolic bottleneck reactions under heat stress.
* **Validation against 13C MFA**: Allows direct statistical validation of simulated fluxes against experimental $^{13}\text{C}$ Metabolic Flux Analysis literature data, computing Pearson correlation $r$ and RMSE.

---

## 2. Biophysical Thermodynamic Binding Model

The platform has evolved from qualitative classification to quantitative thermodynamic binding predictions:
* **Shea-Ackers Formulation**: Integrates a sequence-specific thermodynamic partition function model on the backend.
* **Affinity Constants**: Evaluates the absolute binding free energy ($\Delta G_{\mathrm{bind}}$ in $\text{kcal/mol}$) and estimated thermodynamic dissociation constant ($K_d$ in $\text{nM}$) for TFs on scanned promoter DNA sequences.
* **Prioritization Scores**: Random Forest predicted probabilities continue to represent regulatory prioritization confidence based on evidence matrices (motif presence, ChIP-seq, expression correlation, and operon structures).

---

## 3. Requirement for Experimental Validation

> [!IMPORTANT]
> All computational predictions, dynamic simulations, and thermodynamic affinities generated by Cgl Regulation Explorer serve as high-throughput screening hypotheses to streamline metabolic engineering target selection. They must be verified via wet-lab molecular and metabolic characterization.
